Introduction to JavaScript

Discover the fascinating journey of JavaScript, from its rapid development to becoming a cornerstone of the modern web.

The Origin Story

Brendan Eich developed JavaScript in just 10 days for Netscape Navigator 2.0, marking the start of an era in 1995.

JavaScript: The Third Language of the Web

After HTML and CSS, JavaScript emerged as the scripting language that added interactivity to the static web.

JScript and Browser Wars

Microsoft's introduction of JScript sparked the browser wars, leading to compatibility issues that plagued developers.

Standardization by ECMA

The European Computer Manufacturers Association (now Ecma International) stepped in to standardize the language as ECMAScript, bringing order to the chaos.

Evolution of ECMAScript

From the first version in 1997 to the modern "living standard" approach with yearly updates, ECMAScript has continuously evolved to meet the web's growing needs.

Why History?

Understanding JavaScript's history helps us appreciate the reasons behind its design decisions and quirks.

Luckily, you don't need to know the history of JavaScript to be able to use it, or to get a job in it. Where it helps to know the history is in understanding why certain things are the way they are.

Why History?

The example of ES4 tells answers the question you may have: why don't they just fix it?

Making a breaking change to a language specification means that all existing code may stop working.

This is a bad idea: we want our browsers to be backwards compatible, and so the ECMAScript specifications should be.

Learning JavaScript Today

Despite its complex history, mastering JavaScript opens doors to modern web development, without needing to dwell on past browser wars or version conflicts.
